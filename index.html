The Challenge

LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) batteries have an extremely flat voltage curve. The voltage barely changes across most of the charge range, making State of Charge (SoC) estimation incredibly difficult. Traditional methods require months of expensive lab testing. I solved this using only noisy, real-world operational data.

Multi-Parameter Identifiability

Solved the fundamental challenge of parameter identification in systems with flat observability and circular interdependencies through an iterative approach and physical laws.

Extended Kalman Filter (EKF) + Equivalent Circuit Model (ECM)

Implemented sensor fusion combining current integration and voltage measurements through an EKF and ECM voltage simulation.

  • Prediction via process model (Coulomb counting)
  • Update via measurement model + ECM
  • Correction based on uncertainty
Iterative Model Creation

Developed a novel methodology for creating battery models from chaotic operational data:

  • Time-dependent parameter fitting
  • Resistance temperature and current dependency modeling
  • SoC dependency extraction from residuals
Zero Lab Testing Required

Unlike traditional approaches requiring months in climate chambers, this system learns from real-world conditions. The developed model outperforms literature examples by being truly fit for the application.
